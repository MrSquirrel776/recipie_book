Spaghetti Bolognese

Description

Spaghetti Bolognese, a beloved Italian classic, is a hearty and flavorsome pasta dish cherished worldwide. At its core, it features al dente spaghetti noodles coated in a rich, savory Bolognese sauce. The sauce, often simmered for hours to develop its depth of flavor, typically consists of finely minced or ground beef cooked with aromatic vegetables like onions, carrots, and celery. This robust base is enhanced with a generous amount of tomato sauce or puree, lending a vibrant red hue and tangy sweetness to the dish. Seasonings such as garlic, bay leaves, oregano, and basil are commonly added, infusing the sauce with an irresistible aroma and taste. The dish is typically crowned with a sprinkling of freshly grated Parmesan cheese or chopped parsley, adding a final touch of freshness and richness to the ensemble.

Served piping hot and garnished with a flourish of grated Parmesan cheese, Spaghetti Bolognese embodies the perfect marriage of simplicity and comfort. Its allure lies not only in its mouthwatering flavors but also in its ability to evoke feelings of warmth and nostalgia. Whether enjoyed as a cozy family dinner or shared among friends at a bustling trattoria, each twirl of spaghetti laden with the savory Bolognese sauce delivers a symphony of textures and tastes that never fail to satisfy. This timeless classic transcends cultural boundaries, uniting food lovers around the globe in appreciation of its humble yet exquisite charm.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti pasta
  • Ground beef
  • Onion
  • Carrot
  • Celery
  • Garlic
  • Tomato sauce or puree
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Dried oregano
  • Dried basil
  • Bay leaves
  • Red wine (optional)
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (for serving)
  • Fresh parsley (for garnish)

Method

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add diced onions, carrots, and celery to the skillet. Cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add minced garlic and cook for an additional 1 minute.
  4. Add ground beef to the skillet, breaking it up with a spoon. Cook until browned and no longer pink.
  5. Stir in tomato sauce or puree, along with dried oregano, dried basil, bay leaves, salt, and black pepper.
  6. If using, pour in red wine and let it simmer for a few minutes to enhance the flavor.
  7. Reduce the heat to low and let the sauce simmer, uncovered, for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  8. Meanwhile, cook spaghetti noodles according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  9. Once the sauce has thickened and flavors have developed, remove the bay leaves and adjust seasoning if necessary.
  10. Serve the Bolognese sauce over cooked spaghetti noodles, garnished with grated Parmesan cheese and chopped fresh parsley.
