Classic Lasagne

Description

Lasagne, an Italian culinary masterpiece, is a dish that embodies layers of flavor and texture. At its heart lies thin sheets of pasta, delicately crafted to provide a sturdy yet yielding foundation. These pasta layers are generously interspersed with a savory blend of ingredients, typically including rich tomato sauce, creamy béchamel, and a hearty filling of minced meat, such as beef, pork, or lamb. Each layer contributes its distinct essence, creating a symphony of taste that unfolds with every bite. Topped with a golden crust of melted cheese, lasagne emerges from the oven as a tantalizing fusion of tender pasta, robust sauce, and indulgent cheese, enticing the senses with its aroma and promising a gratifying dining experience.

The allure of lasagne extends beyond its gastronomic appeal; it symbolizes tradition, warmth, and communal dining. Originating from the Italian region of Emilia-Romagna, lasagne has evolved into a beloved classic cherished worldwide. Its preparation often involves a labor of love, with families and chefs alike dedicating time and attention to crafting each layer with care. Whether enjoyed as a comforting meal shared with loved ones or served as a centerpiece at celebratory gatherings, lasagne embodies the essence of Italian hospitality, inviting all who partake to savor the harmony of its flavors and the richness of its heritage.

Ingredients

  • Onion, garlic, carrot and celery - for the flavour base, a soffrito;
  • Beef
  • Canned tomato and tomato paste
  • Seasonings - beef bouillon cubes (stock cubes), bay leaves, thyme, oregano, Worcestershire sauce
  • Butter
  • Flour
  • Milk
  • Cheese
  • Lasagne sheets

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Boil lasagna sheets according to package instructions until al dente.
  3. While sheets cook, prepare the meat sauce. Sauté ground beef, onions, and garlic in a pan until browned.
  4. Add tomato sauce, diced tomatoes, tomato paste, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper to the beef mixture. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. In a separate bowl, mix together ricotta cheese, egg, grated Parmesan cheese, and chopped parsley.
  6. Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom of a baking dish.
  7. Layer lasagna sheets on top of the sauce, followed by a layer of the ricotta mixture, then shredded mozzarella cheese.
  8. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used, ending with a layer of mozzarella cheese on top.
  9. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes.
  10. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 25 minutes until the cheese is bubbly and golden.
